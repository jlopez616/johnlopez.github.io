I am a senior computer science major and a Ronald E. McNair Scholar at Loyola Marymount University, a Jesuit college in Los Angeles, California.

This fall I will be attending the University of California, Irvine, to begin a Ph.D. program in Education. My advisor will be Dr. June Ahn.

My Jesuit education had a strong emphasis on service, which requires a strong understanding of the needs of the communities one serves with and for. This service foundation is what drives me to do research - I believe that in order to create real solutions to facilitate learning, it’s necessary to understand how previous systems may have addressed or failed to address the social, economic, or cultural underlying issues in a community. I desire to make education and technology more accessible to those around the world, and I aim to have my work help bridge the social and economic barriers that prevent others from fulfilling their dreams.

Software Development Projects

Ballona Discovery

For my senior project, I am collaborating with several LMU students to develop Ballona Discovery, a content management system for the Ballona Discovery Park. Park staff and volunteers will be able to use a web based interface to design and publish interpretive and interactive mobile web pages - without writing a single line of code! These pages will be visible through "tours" as guests walk through the park.

The project will be launched in early summer, 2020. You may view its demo at our website.

StoneScript

StoneScript is an esoteric programming language themed - it is the oldest programming language ever discovered! Written in JavaScript using the Ohm library, StoneScript compiles "stone tablets" written using the "most primitive" syntax into JavaScript.

To learn more about StoneScript, please visit the StoneScript website.

Research Experience

Novice Debugging in Block-Based and Hybrid Environments

This was the research project I completed as a participant of the Intelligent Multimodal Human-Computer Interaction Research Experience for Undergraduates (REU) at the University of Florida. This project, in collaboration with the LearnDialogue Group, investigated the abilites and perceptions of novice computer science students when debugging programs using Block-Based and Hybrid environments.

You may view our poster here.

GRNsight

GRNsight is a web application created by Dr. Kam Dahlquist and Dr. John David Dionisio at Loyola Marymount University for visualizing gene regulatory networks. As a member of the GRNsight development team, I performed several debugging tasks to improve the robustness of the software, and I implemented an interactive Gene Pages feature.

You may access GRNsight here.

Extracurricular Engagement

Agapé Service Organization

I was a member of the Agapé Service Organization, a community that emphasizes raising mental health awareness and endling the stigma surrounding mental illness through service and accompaniment. I believe that in order to do service, it’s necessary to understand the needs of the communities I serve with and for. As the former Vice President of Service, I led a committee to create and facilitate educational activities for the after school program at Our Lady of Victory Elementary and Middle School. I also co-lead a peer to peer mentoring group for first year students known as Agapé Allies.

Academic Community of Excellence

The Academic Community of Excellence is a living-learning community for underrepresented college students who aspire to attend graduate or professional school. I participated in the program as a sophomore and helped coordinate monthly social engagements. During my junior year, I became a Peer Scholar/Teaching Assistant, where I helped teach by LIBA 1001 and 1002 by facilitating discussions, giving presentations, leading activities, and providing support to the students inside and outside the classroom to enhance their education. I've I have also developed lesson plans and created activities for these classes.

826LA

I was a writing tutor/homework aide for 826LA, a non-profit after school tutoring organization in Los Angeles, CA. I work with students from first to sixth grade in groups of two to five with tasks such as reading comprehension, writing support, and individualized homework help. Every day at 826LA presents me opportunity to learn how to adapt my methods of support to the needs of the individual - a skill I intend to bring into designing future learning systems.

ELS Malibu Youth Camp

Over the summer of my sophomore, I worked as a Residential Youth Counselor for a summer camp that supported over 200 multilingual campers, ages 9-18, studying English. I supported the campers with English grammar and vocabulary through individual group conversations at mealtimes and periods of downtime. I also created and facilitated afternoon arts and sports activities such as s’mores, movie nights, and basketball for parties up to 50.
