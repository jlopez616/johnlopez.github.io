Course Objectives

In this course, students will be introduced to "educational data science", a new, rapidly growing field that brings together the brightest minds of mathematics, computing, and education to solve real-world problems in teaching, learning, and educational improvement. While this course will introduce educational data science more broadly, we will use the context of the Fraction Ball: Out of Bounds video game as a motivating example. This course may be understood through 3 parts:

Part 1: Building Blocks, Weeks 1-3

In this part of the course, students develop a surface level understanding of data science and the study of learning. Specifically, students will look at how learning is understood in a variety of contexts, and how data science allows researchers to uncover patterns, predictions, and hidden structures within these contexts. In addition, students will be introduced to the Python programming language for data analysis, run basic descriptive statistics, and create simple visualizations with simple datasets.

Part 2: Going Deeper, Weeks 4-7

In this part of the course, students will gain a deeper understanding of how educational data science may be used to study latent, or hidden, phenomena. Specifically, students will be able to focus on executive functions in context, and how data collected from video games may be used to study that context. Students will also get more experience with data processing through Python, and practice describing results to others.

Part 3: Tying it All Together, Weeks 7-10

In this part of the course, students will create their own datasets using the Fraction Ball: Out of Bounds video game. Students will work together, in groups of 4, to apply the data analysis techniques learned from the course to clean, visualize, describe, and produce a simple model of an executive function of their choice. Students will present their findings during week 10 of the course.
